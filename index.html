QUESTION

Australia's changing age (Population ageing)

Older people make up a considerable proportion of Australia’s population.In 2017, there were 3.8 million Australians aged 65 and over (comprising 15% of the total population) —increasing from 319,000 (5%) in 1927 and 1.3 million (9%) in 1977 . The number and proportion of older Australians is expected to continue to grow. By 2057, it is projected there will be 8.8 million older people in Australia (22% of the population); by 2097, 12.8 million people (25%) will be aged 65 and over .

It is an important social and economic challenge facing Australia(Sydney) that more and more elderly people appear. If those old people's life and health are not good, they can't continue to contribute socially, culturally and economically to the wider community, and finally in Australia (Sydney) there will be a huge loss.

THE SOLUTION PROPOSAL

In order to better protect the lives of these old people, I chose to build several old people's houses at the intersection of Burton and Victoria street, where there is a hospital and a small park just across the road.If the old man has some physical problems, they can get to the hospital quickly, so the old man can get treatment in the shortest time.And if some old people want to go for a walk, they can also get to green park quickly.The building also has a gym and reading room, so that old people can exercise in bad weather and increase knowledge in their spare time.There is also a small botanical garden on the roof of the building, with grass and small trees, on which old people can roast and bask.What's more, the building is equipped with some professional nursing staff to take care of those old people who are physically handicapped.
